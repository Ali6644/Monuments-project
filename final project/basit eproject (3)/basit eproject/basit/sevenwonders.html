The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications and walls that were built across the northern borders of China to protect the Chinese states and empires from invasions by various nomadic groups and military incursions. It is one of the most recognizable and famous man-made structures in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Chichén Itzá

Chichen Itza is a significant archaeological site located in the Yucatan Peninsula of Mexico. It was once a thriving pre-Columbian city and is now one of the most visited and well-preserved ancient Maya sites in Mexico. Chichen Itza is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and was declared one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007.

Petra

Petra is an ancient archaeological site located in southern Jordan. It is one of the most famous and significant archaeological sites in the world, renowned for its rock-cut architecture and historical importance. Petra was the capital city of the Nabateans, an ancient Arab civilization that flourished in the region from around the 4th century BC to the 2nd century AD.

Machu Picchu

It is perched above the Urubamba River valley in a narrow saddle between two sharp peaks—Machu Picchu (“Old Peak”) and Huayna Picchu (“New Peak”)—at an elevation of 7,710 feet (2,350 metres). One of the few major pre-Columbian ruins found nearly intact, Machu Picchu was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor) is a 30 metre high statue, mounted upon an 8 metre pedestal, and of course the 700 metre high mountain of Corcovado. It was constructed between 1922 and 1931 from soapstone and reinforced concrete. Today it is a Brazilian cultural icon and global symbol of Christianity.

The Colosseum

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient Roman amphitheater located in the heart of Rome, Italy. It is one of the most iconic and well-preserved structures from ancient times and stands as a testament to the architectural and engineering achievements of the Roman Empire.

The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic and world-renowned mausoleum located in Agra, India. It is one of the most beautiful and cherished architectural masterpieces in the world, and it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Taj Mahal is an enduring symbol of love and a testament to the artistic and engineering achievements of the Mughal Empire.
