TIANANMEN SQUARE

Tiananmen Square is an iconic public space located in the heart of Beijing, the capital city of China. It is one of the largest city squares in the world and holds immense historical, cultural, and political significance. Spanning approximately 109 acres (44 hectares), the square serves as a symbol of the country's past, present, and future.

Eternal Golden Castle

Eternal Golden Castle was called "Anping Great Fort" or "Erkunshen Battery" in the ancient time, it was built to resist the Japanese troops who invaded Taiwan due to Mudan incident. When Shen Pao-chen first arriving in Anping in 1874, he believed that Great Forts should be built to protect the city, therefore, the first western style fort was finally completed in 1876, which was designed by French engineers, it was the first port equipped with British Armstrong Cannon in Taiwan, moreover, it was a great step toward modernization of Taiwanese coastguard military defense..

MOUNT FUJI

Mount Fuji, also known as "Fuji-san" in Japanese, is an awe-inspiring stratovolcano located on Honshu, Japan's largest island. Standing tall at 3,776.24 meters (12,389 feet), it is the country's highest peak and a symbol of Japanese identity. Revered for its perfect cone shape and breathtaking beauty, Mount Fuji holds great cultural and spiritual significance. Every year, thousands of visitors from around the world embark on a pilgrimage to summit the majestic mountain.

Mansu Hill Grand

Mansu Hill Grand Monument: A Symbol of National Pride and Unity The Mansu Hill Grand Monument stands as a resplendent testament to the rich history, unwavering spirit, and profound pride of the nation it represents. Nestled in the heart of a majestic hill in the center of the capital city, this colossal structure serves as a prominent landmark and a . Its grandeur, central figures, surrounding landscape, and educational offerings make it a place of deep reverence, reflection, and celebration, embodying the nation's values, aspirations, and shared identity.

Tian Tan Buddha

Tian Tan Buddha: A Majestic Icon of Spiritual Harmon The Tian Tan Buddha, also known as the Big Buddha, is an awe-inspiring symbol of spiritual harmony and cultural heritage, nestled in the lush mountains of Lantau Island, Hong Kong. Towering at an impressive height, this majestic bronze statue draws pilgrims and tourists from around the world, offering a profound spiritual experience and a glimpse into the rich tapestry of Chinese Buddhism.
